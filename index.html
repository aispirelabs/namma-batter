Namma Batter

The term "Namma" is often associated with Bengaluru (Bangalore), India, as it means "ours" or "our" in Kannada. So it could be related to a traditional or regional batter used in Indian cuisine, such as for dosas or idlis.

Best & Good

Fresh, well-fermented batter is the key to achieving the ideal texture. A slightly coarse grind with the right amount of aeration results in soft idlies that feel like cotton and dosas that are perfectly crisp yet tender inside. Whether it’s a busy weekday breakfast or a relaxed weekend brunch, dosa and idly batter is a staple that brings families together over hot plates and delicious accompaniments like chutneys and sambar.
